A little information about the name "Vita Nova"

La Vita Nuova (pronounced [la ˈviːta ˈnwɔːva]; modern Italian for "The New Life") or Vita Nova (Latin and medieval Italian title ) is a tapestry of prose and poetry woven by Dante Alighieri published in 1294. It is not merely a love story — it is the story of love as transcendence. A sacred devotion, immortalized through the figure of Beatrice, who becomes more than muse — she is the embodiment of grace, light, and divine truth. This work is where Dante bleeds his soul onto parchment. Through sonnets and silent longing, he explores the transformation of earthly affection into spiritual awakening. La Vita Nuova is the quiet genesis of what would later bloom into The Divine Comedy — a journey that begins with a gaze and ends in the stars. It is, in essence, a love letter to the ineffable. And perhaps, a gentle reminder that every new life begins with a spark of wonder. Explore more: La Vita Nuova

About

Vita Nova is a web-based puzzle game that brings history back from the dusty shelves and into your hands—one mind-bending riddle at a time. Each puzzle is rooted in a real historical event, idea, or moment—some beautiful, some tragic, all unforgettable. From revolutions to renaissances, secret codes to silent acts of rebellion, this game invites you to not just play through history, but experience it. This isn’t your high school textbook. This is history with heart. With mystery. With purpose. And maybe—just maybe—a few unexpected truths about the world we live in.

Mission

To reignite curiosity for the world’s stories—both the celebrated and the forgotten—through immersive, puzzle-driven experiences that challenge the mind and stir the soul.
